How I Arrived Here

If someone had told me a year ago the ride I'd experience, I'd never have believed them. But when I get an idea in my head I see it through. It was working in the forensics DNA and cancer RNA labs that I started to truly grasp the impact coding has on our world.

I stopped everything I was doing and drew a new path towards coding. At the time I was the Lead RNA Extraction II Technician for a lab focused on Lung, Melanoma, and Prostate cancers.

We were using half million dollar robots in an underperforming manner and that drove me crazy. There was a year and a half waiting list for any code to be changed in our tiny lab and I thought that was unacceptable. I offered to learn, to take on the task of updating, qc'ing, and validating new code that would benefit the patients in turn-around time with results. This was vetoed again and again. I decided to leave to pursue coding so I could make a difference, using computer science.
